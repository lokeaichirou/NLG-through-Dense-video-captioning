 how to cook Mapo tofu get a delicious spicy Chinese meal without calling for take-out this dish which features tofu and pork takes only a few minutes to prepare you will need a quarter cup of chicken broth 2 tablespoons of hot bean paste 2 tablespoons of soy sauce kosher salt 1 pound of regular or soft tofu drained and cut into half inch cubes 2 tablespoons of corn peanut or canola oil half a pound of ground pork shoulder 1 tablespoon plus 1 TSP of finely minced garlic 1 tablespoon plus 1 TSP of finely minced peeled fresh ginger 1 tablespoon of corn starch 1 and 1/2 teaspoons of Japanese sesame oil 1/2 a teaspoon of toasted Szechuan peppercorn powder 3 tablespoons of thinly sliced scallions and steamed rice equipment a wok or large heavy skillet and a sieve step 1 combine the chicken broth bean paste soy sauce and a dash of salt to taste in a small mixing bowl to make a sauce set aside step 2 heat a medium saucepan filled with water until it reaches a simmer reduce heat to low and slide the tofu into the saucepan keep the water at a low simmer while continuing to make the rest of the dish don't let the water reach a full rolling boil boiling the tofu will give it a rubbery feel step 3 heat a wok or large skillet over high heat add one-and-a-half tablespoons corn peanut or canola oil to the wok and swirl it around to coat the skillet step 4 add the ground pork to the heated wok and cook until no longer pink break up clumps of meat as you're cooking add the remaining half a tablespoon of oil to the meat if it begins to stick to the wok step 5 add the garlic and ginger to the wok and continue cooking for an additional two minutes stir in your reserved sauce and return the mixture to a simmer sprinkle garlic cloves with a dash of salt while mincing to keep them from sticking to your knife step 6 drain the poached tofu using a sieve and add it to the wok stir gently to combine step 7 dissolve the cornstarch in two tablespoons of water add to the wok and bring to a boil stir gently cook until the mixture has thickened and become glossy about 15 seconds step 8 remove the wok or skillet from the heat and sprinkle with sesame oil Szechuan peppercorn powder and 2 tablespoons of scallions stir until the ingredients are combined sprinkle with the remaining scallions and serve present with a bowl of steamed rice for a tasty Chinese meal that's sure to be a crowd pleaser did you know soy beans which are used to make tofu were first planted in colonial America in 1755 in Savannah Georgia
