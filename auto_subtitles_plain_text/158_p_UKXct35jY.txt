 just about anything shoved on a stick and grilled is instantly more fun to eat and these beef shish kabobs are a fast and flavorful recipe to slap on the grill here's how I make them first you'll need to cut up the meat take a 1 and a half to 2 pound sirloin steak trim off any fat or sinew and cut it into 1-inch cubes I like to use sirloin because it has beefy flavor and is well marbled but still tender next make the marinade whisk together 1 tablespoon kosher salt 2 teaspoons light brown sugar 1 tsp lemon zest half a teaspoon cumin 1/2 a teaspoon of paprika 1/4 teaspoon of pepper and a quarter teaspoon of coriander then add 1/3 cup of olive oil 2 tablespoons of tomato paste and 5 finely chopped garlic cloves to the bowl and whisk it to combine transfer 1/4 cup of the marinade to a zip top bag and add the beef massage the marinade in with your hands and let the beef sit out while you prep the vegetables cut up one pound of summer squash and one red onion into 1 inch chunks then clean and send eight ounces of cremini mushrooms you can use all kinds of vegetables just make sure they're cut the same size so they finish cooking at the same time also if you want to use vegetables they take a little bit longer to cook like new potatoes or corn on the cob you want to par cook them before you add them to the skewers add all the veggies to the bowl with the marinade and toss them around until they are coated heat your grill to medium-high and while the grill is heating up thread the marinated beef and vegetables separately onto 10 12-inch skewers now you may see meat and vegetables skewered together at the butcher case but they definitely don't belong in the same skewer the reason is that the meat will finish cooking before the vegetables and you want to be able to remove them from the grill while the vegetables continue to cook that way you won't end up with overcooked meat or undercooked vegetables when the grill is ready put the skewers on close the grill and cook turning the skewers every 2 to 3 minutes take the beef skewers off when they are lightly charred all over in an instant-read thermometer measures 125 to 130 degrees for medium-rare that should take about 6 to 8 minutes cover the skewers loosely with foil to keep them warm continue to cook the vegetable skewers turning them every few minutes until they are lightly charred all over and crisp tender about 6 to 8 minutes more now you're ready to serve this beef shish kebab recipe is a fast easy and flavorful way to make a complete meal on the grill the tomato paste and brown sugar in the marinade help the beef and vegetables Brown and the cumin and coriander give it Middle Eastern flavor and that's it the easiest way to make beef shish kebab you and you
